    A Kid Again

    Where did time go? Was Astro Bot a temporal vortex in disguise? Is this how playing in those McDonald's ball pits from the 90s used to feel? And where did the weekend go?

    There is so much joy in this game. Among what felt like a thousand unexpected highlights, rapidly happening one after the other... maybe my strongest dork reaction came from the sudden appearance of the McDohl bot—totally out of the blue, as in a first playthrough of Suikoden II. It felt like Christmas, a birthday present, and some sort of climax to many years of playing.

    Recruiting the 108 Stars of Destiny is—to this day—still on my bucket list, but with Astro I just couldn’t stop and ended up collecting everything. I left my memory card with aching hands after a final challenge that kicked my ass... but also with a rekindled desire to finish my abandoned run through the Uncharted series. The astro-remix of Nate's Theme actually made me emotional.

    This was also the end of an improvised trilogy of 3D platformers. And I guess I can see myself going after the few Figments I missed in Psychonauts 2... but... Mario Odyssey's 999 Power Moons?

    I would need another time vortex. Or retirement.
